Verbs: present tense

Most verbs follow this pattern:
  • ik loop
  • jij loopt (loop jij?)
  • u loopt (loopt u?)
  • hij/zij/het loopt
  • wij lopen
  • jullie lopen
  • zij lopen

Possesive pronouns

Pronouns

I think you are quite familiar with the below pronouns at this point.
As a reminder, here they are:

  • I - ik
  • you (singular) - jij
  • you (singular, polite) - u
  • he/she - hij/zij
  • we - wij (we)
  • you (plural) - jullie
  • they - zij (ze)

When you want to make it clear that something or someone belongs to someone else, you can use possesive pronouns. They work in the same way as in English.

  • my - mijn
  • your (singular) - jouw
  • your (singular, polite) - uw
  • his/her: zijn/haar
  • our - ons/onze
  • your (plural) - jullie
  • their - hun

Note that all these are used when the object being possessed comes right after it. This is usually a thing or a concept, but can also be an animal or a person.

Examples:

  • mijn hond
  • zijn vrouw
  • hun auto
  • jullie idee
  • onze leraar (teacher)
  • jouw vogel
  • uw mening (opinion)

Notes

  • We don't really have a translation for 'its'.

  • Even if we don't know the gender, Dutch people often just call animals a 'hij' or a 'zij', not a 'het'. This especially when it concerns smarter and bigger animals. It is more likely that a fly will be called a 'het' than a pet dog.

  • A lot of Dutch people make mistakes when writing the possesive pronouns 'jouw' and 'uw'. They will omit the 'w', because they don't remember the rules. If you do this right, you're already doing better than quite a few Dutch people :)

Ons, onze

Whether we use 'ons' or 'onze' depends on the noun that comes after it.
If we use the article 'de' in front of a noun, we use 'onze'.
If we use the article 'het' in front of a noun, we use 'ons'.

Examples:

  • De kat: onze kat
  • De boot: onze boot
  • De wereld: onze wereld
  • Het paard: ons paard
  • Het gordijn: ons gordijn (curtain)
  • Het tijdschrift: ons tijdschrift (magazine)

When you make a noun smaller by adding '-je', "-tje" or "-pje" to the end, the article being used is always 'het'.

Examples:

  • Het katje: ons katje
  • Het bootje: ons bootje
  • Het wereldje: ons wereldje
  • Het paardje: ons paardje
  • Het gordijntje: ons gordijntje
  • Het tijdschriftje: ons tijdschriftje

When you have more than one of a certain noun, the article is always 'de'.


Examples:

  • De katten: onze katten
  • De boten: onze boten
  • De werelden: onze werelden
  • De paarden: onze paarden
  • De gordijnen: onze gordijnen
  • De tijdschriften: onze tijdschriften

Even if you make something smaller by putting -je behind it, it will still become de/onze when it turns plural.

Examples:

  • de muis-het muisje-de muisjes: onze muis-ons muisje-onze muisjes
  • de tuin-het tuintje-de tuintjes: onze tuin-ons tuintje-onze tuintjes
